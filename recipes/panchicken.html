Pan-Seared Chicken Breasts

Recipe Description

As an attempt to be healthier this year, I tried my best to learn a few different chicken breast recipes, especially when I was cutting weight down. Although commonly hated for its dry texture, a well-cooked chicken breast can be remarkably juicy and delicious as well.

The key to this, however, is using a meat thermometer. Any cheap one will do, but it is the easiest way to avoid the dry, tasteless texture that chicken breasts are commonly known for. I hope you all can enjoy this simple and quick way to cook an outstanding chicken breast!

Ingredients

  • 2-3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • olive or other oil
  • stainless or cast-iron pan
  • meat thermometer
  • kosher salt to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • smoked paprika (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to ~190 Celsius / 375 Fahrenheit.
  2. Place pan on highest heat available (and turn on oven fan!)
  3. Pat chicken breasts dry with a paper towel, then season generously with salt, pepper, and paprika if using.
  4. When pan is rippingly hot, add a few splashes of oil, carefully place chicken in pan, and start a timer.
  5. When timer reaches 2 minutes, carefully flip chicken pieces over and turn off heat.
  6. Very carefully transfer pan (with chicken) into the preheated oven.
  7. Check the temperature after total time of 6 minutes! The temperature for a fully-cooked chicken breast is 160 F. When testing, make sure to probe the largest, thickest part of each piece.
  8. As the chicken's internal temperature will continue to raise while resting, you may pull the pieces when they reach around 155 F.
  9. When the chicken reaches the target temperature, take the pieces out of the pan and place on a deep plate or bowl to cool.
  10. After allowing the chicken to rest a few minutes, enjoy!! Don't forget to save/reserve/use all the juice that comes out as well!
